Boerenkool

Boerenkool is a traditional Dutch dish, which is typically enjoyed in the wintertime, i could write an entire article on it, but as many Dutch food is meant to be: No embellishment needed, cook and let the dish speak for itself.

Ingredients:

  • 500g Potatoes
  • 500g Boerenkool
  • 250g Bacon rashers
  • 1 whole Rookworst

So, how do we make it?

  1. Put the potatoes in a big pot, fill with water until potatoes are just about submerged.
  2. Put the boerenkool on top and cook for about 10 minutes.
  3. After 10 minutes add the rookworst on top, and let it sit on medium heat for 20 more minutes.
  4. Take the pan off the stove and mash the potatoes and boerenkool.
  5. Take a skillet and bake the rashers until golden brown, add these to the mash.
  6. Finally, put the mash in a nice bowl, or leave in the big pot, put it all on the table, put the rookworst on top and enjoy!
